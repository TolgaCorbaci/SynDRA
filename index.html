How it works

The same drug, scattered across names and identifiers — reassembled.

SynDRA integrates drug synonyms from KatDB, TTD, PRISM, and LINCS 2020, then anchors every name to a standardized InChIKey so that salts, solvates, and stereoisomers of one molecule all resolve to the same canonical compound.

01

Harmonize names

Synonyms, brand names, and identifiers from four sources are normalized and resolved to a single canonical node — keyed by a stable SynDRA ID, not by BRD. Entries with no structure are retained as name-only nodes rather than dropped.

02

Anchor to structure

Each compound is standardized with RDKit: desalted, charge-neutralized, and assigned a full InChIKey. Structural variants (salts, solvates, stereoforms) share a common connectivity skeleton and are linked automatically.

03

Bridge to L1000 & enrichment

BRD identifiers, PubChem CIDs, and TTD IDs are cross-references — all xrefs for variants are surfaced together, so one query recovers every L1000 signature and enrichment library member for the same molecule.
